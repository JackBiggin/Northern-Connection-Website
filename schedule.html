Schedule

10:30

Panel: Alternative Voices

Featuring Jo Meek (Prison Radio Association), Grace Holliday (The Guardian/Freelancer) & Tulip Mazumdar (Global Health Correspondent) Location The Riley Smith Theatre

In the media, there are dominant voices that are heard above others, and this panel will address this issue. The panellists will discuss their experiences in the media, and the importance of hearing different voices.

11:30

Keynote: Jo Meek (Prison Radio Association)

Featuring Jo Meek (Prison Radio Association) Location The Riley Smith Theatre

Jo is a producer for the Prison Radio Association, who is based in HMP YOI Styal. There, she produces the Fanily Request Show and Primetime. Jo has produced for all the BBC's terrestrial UK networks, and the BBC world service. She's the co-director of Sparklab which is a Manchester based production company, and has recently produced Women's Hour. Jo is going to be talking about her exciting career, and how she got to where she is.

12:30

Panel: Alternative Avenues Into The Media

Featuring Josh Aitman (Melodic Distraction), Sally Walker (Sofar Sounds Leeds) & James Ewing (Prison Radio Association/BBC/University of Salford) Location The Riley Smith Theatre

This panel is going to showcase the different jobs that are available to students in student media societies. It's going to look beyond the classic presenter/producer roles, and showcase some more leftfield ideas. Students who are sure about where they want to work in the media, or who know they want to look beyond radio/television should attend.

1:30

Lunch

A free cold buffet of sandwiches and snacks is included in the price of your ticket.

2:30

Panel: Multimedia Platforms

Featuring Grace Holliday (The Guardian/Freelancer), Mo Ayoub (Radar Radio) & Rhys Hancock (MCR Live) Location Pyramid Cafe

The world of media is always changing, and it's becoming increasingly important that students are able to work across multiple types of media in the industry. This panel will discuss how social media has changed the industry, as well as how the TV and radio industries have been affected.

Panel: Student Media into Professional Media

Featuring James Kenny (WiseBuddah), Jess Dawson (BBC/ITN), Ben Cooper (BBC), Sophy Crane (Hawk Eye) & Eve Betts Location Pyramid Theatre

This panel is going to talk about how to move from Student Media societies, into the professional industry. It will illuminate to students the steps they should take next, and what they should expect to do. Students who have questions about what steps to take to move into the industry should attend.

3:30

Panel: The Future of Radio

Featuring Sam van Geffen (Capital), Sophie Sviensson (XS Manchester), Rhys Hancock (MCR Live) & Chris North (North Media Talent) Location Pyramid Theatre

The radio industry is constantly evolving, and recent technological advancements have accelerated this. This panel is going to discuss where radio is headed next, and the recent technical developments that could change it.

Panel: Student Media Round Table

Featuring Representatives of LSR, Forge and LSRadio Location Pyramid Cafe

This is a chance for students from across all three stations to come together and share what they know. The discussion is going to be podcasted, for future student radios to use. All are welcome.

4:30

Networking Session

Finish off the day with a relaxed drink and the opportunity to chat to any of the panelists or students, and ask them any questions you have.

5:30

DJ Set

LSR DJs will be spinning tracks to finish the day.
